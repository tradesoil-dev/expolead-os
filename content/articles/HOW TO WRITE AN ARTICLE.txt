HOW TO WRITE AN ARTICLE FOR THE RESOURCES PAGE
==============================================

Drop your finished Word file in this folder, then tell Claude
"new article ready" and it goes live.


WHAT TO PUT IN THE WORD DOC
---------------------------

1. TITLE
   First line of the document. Just type it as normal text.
   Make it specific and useful, not clever.

   Good:  How to qualify a buyer in 5 minutes at your booth
   Weak:  Booth tips


2. INTRO PARAGRAPH
   Second block. Two or three sentences.
   Say what the reader will be able to do after reading it.


3. SECTION HEADINGS
   Use Word's Heading 2 style, or just type the heading on its
   own line and leave a blank line above and below it.
   Numbered headings work well: "1. Log the lead before you leave"


4. BODY PARAGRAPHS
   Write normally. Blank line between paragraphs.
   Short paragraphs read better on a phone. Three or four
   sentences is plenty.


5. BULLET LISTS
   Use Word's bullet button, or start each line with a dash.


THAT IS ALL THE FORMATTING THERE IS.
No bold, italics, tables, images or colours are needed. They are
ignored on conversion, so do not spend time on them.


HOUSE STYLE
-----------

- No em dashes and no hyphens used as dashes. Use commas.
- Say "connections" not "leads" where you can. That is what the
  app calls them.
- Lead with the reader's problem, then the answer.
- Write the way you talk to a customer, not the way a brochure
  talks.
- Never invent a statistic, a customer quote or a company name.
  If you want a number in there, tell Claude and it will find a
  real sourced one or leave it out.


NAMING THE FILE
---------------

Name it after the article so it is easy to find later.

  qualify-a-buyer-at-your-booth.docx
  choosing-which-shows-to-attend.docx


THREE THINGS CLAUDE NEEDS FROM YOU EACH TIME
--------------------------------------------

Send these with "new article ready", or Claude will pick and
tell you what it chose:

1. TOPIC. One of: Exhibitions, Sales, Marketing, Follow-up
2. FEATURED? Yes or no. Featured shows at the top of /resources.
   Only one article should be featured at a time.
3. PUBLISH DATE. Defaults to the day you send it.


WHAT HAPPENS NEXT
-----------------

Claude reads the Word file, converts it, adds it to the site,
and pushes it live. It will send you the live link to check.

Reading time is calculated automatically. You do not need to
work it out.

Anything that looks wrong after it is live, say so and it gets
fixed in a minute. Nothing here is permanent.


ONE LIMITATION TO KNOW
----------------------

Articles go live when Claude runs the conversion, not the moment
you save the file here. If you need to publish without waiting,
that is the admin publishing page, which is a separate build.
